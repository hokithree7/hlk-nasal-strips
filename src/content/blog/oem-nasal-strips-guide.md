Launching your own nasal strip brand is one of the most lucrative opportunities in the consumer health space — if you get the manufacturing partnership right. Here's everything you need to know about OEM nasal strip production in China.

## Step 1: Define Your Product

Before contacting factories, decide on:

- **Target market:** This determines certifications needed (FDA for US, CE for EU, etc.)
- **Price positioning:** Budget, mid-market, or premium? This affects material and packaging choices.
- **Unique selling proposition:** What makes your brand different? Better adhesion? Natural ingredients? Eco-friendly packaging?

## Step 2: Choose the Right Factory

Not all factories are equal. Look for:

- **Certifications:** FDA registration, ISO 13485, CE marking — non-negotiable for serious importers.
- **Experience:** 10+ years in medical adhesives is ideal. Ask for client references.
- **R&D capability:** Can they develop custom formulations or just produce standard items?
- **Communication:** English-speaking account managers save enormous time and prevent specification errors.
- **Minimum order quantity (MOQ):** Should match your initial budget. HLK starts at 1,000 boxes for standard products.

## Step 3: The Sampling Process

Never skip this step. Here's the proper sampling workflow:

1. Send your specification requirements (size, adhesion level, material, color, packaging concept).
2. Factory produces 3-5 prototype versions (3-5 days at HLK).
3. Test samples yourself: wear them, sleep with them, exercise with them. Give them to colleagues for feedback.
4. Provide specific feedback — "needs 20% more adhesion" is more helpful than "not sticky enough."
5. Factory adjusts and sends revised samples. Iterate until perfect.

## Step 4: Packaging Development

Your packaging must satisfy three goals: shelf appeal, regulatory compliance, and product protection. Key decisions:

- Individual sachet material (foil for maximum protection, paper for eco-positioning)
- Box count (10, 30, 50, or 100 — 30-count is global bestseller)
- Display features (peg hole, counter display, shelf tray)
- Required labeling (ingredients, warnings, instructions, barcode, country of origin)

## Step 5: Production and Quality Control

Once samples are approved and packaging is finalized:

1. Factory produces your order (7-15 days for standard quantities).
2. Request pre-shipment inspection (we recommend third-party inspection for first orders).
3. Factory provides QC documentation: adhesion test reports, dimension reports, packaging integrity reports.
4. Arrange shipping: FOB, CIF, or DDP depending on your preference.

## Step 6: Logistics and Import

Sea freight is most cost-effective for orders over 50 boxes. Air freight for urgent restocking. Express couriers (DHL/UPS/FedEx) for samples and small initial orders. Work with a customs broker for first-time imports — they handle FDA clearance, duties, and documentation.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

- **Skipping certification verification:** Always ask for current certificates (they expire). Verify FDA registration number online.
- **Ordering too much packaging:** Start with smaller packaging runs in case you need design changes.
- **Not testing in your climate:** Adhesive performs differently in humid vs. dry environments.
- **Rushing sampling:** The biggest cost in OEM is getting the specification wrong. Take your time with samples.
