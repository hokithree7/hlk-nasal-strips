As a retailer or distributor in the respiratory health space, you're likely evaluating whether to stock nasal strips, nasal sprays, or both. Here's our data-driven comparison to inform your product strategy.

## Market Size and Growth

Nasal strips: $8.2B market (2025), growing at 8.2% CAGR. Nasal sprays: $28.6B market (2025), growing at 5.4% CAGR. While sprays dominate in absolute size, nasal strips are growing significantly faster — presenting a compelling opportunity for early-mover retailers.

## Consumer Preference Comparison

| Factor | Nasal Strips | Nasal Sprays |
|--------|-------------|--------------|
| Drug-free | Yes | Often contains medication |
| Side effects | Minimal (skin only) | Rebound congestion possible |
| Duration | 8-12 hours | 4-6 hours typical |
| Reusable | Single use | Multi-dose bottle |
| Sports use | Excellent | Impractical during exercise |
| Price point | $0.15-0.40 per strip | $0.05-0.15 per dose |
| Regulatory | Class I medical device | OTC drug (varies) |

## Why Retailers Are Adding Nasal Strips

Three key reasons:

1. **Complementary, not competitive:** Most consumers use both products for different situations. Strips for sleep and exercise, sprays for daytime congestion relief. Stocking both increases basket size.
2. **Higher margin potential:** Nasal strips have 45-60% retail margins, compared to 30-40% for branded sprays. Private label strips can achieve even higher margins.
3. **Growing awareness:** Athlete endorsements, social media trends, and sleep health awareness are driving consumer interest in drug-free breathing solutions.

## Retail Channel Performance

Nasal strips perform well across multiple channels:

- **Pharmacies:** 40% of sales. Trust factor is highest here.
- **Sports retailers:** 25% of sales. Fastest growing channel.
- **E-commerce:** 20% of sales. Amazon dominates, but branded sites are growing.
- **Supermarkets:** 10% of sales. Impulse purchase driven.
- **Specialty (sleep clinics, ENT):** 5% of sales. High-value, loyal customers.
