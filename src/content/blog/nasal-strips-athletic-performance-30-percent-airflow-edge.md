## The Airflow Bottleneck Most Athletes Ignore

During high-intensity exercise, inhalation force can be strong enough to collapse the internal nasal valves. When those walls buckle inward, airflow drops and the athlete is forced into oral breathing sooner than the lungs actually require. Oral breathing:

- Skips the humidification and filtration the nasal cavity provides
- Reduces nitric oxide production
- Accelerates dehydration and throat irritation
- Raises perceived exertion at the same workload

[HLK nasal strips](/products#nasal-strips) address this at the source with a physical mechanism, not a stimulant.

## How the HLK Bone-Shape Strip Works

Our signature 66x19mm bone-shaped (H-shape) design places embedded high-tensile spring bands across the nasal bridge. Once applied, the bands seek to return to their flat shape and mechanically lift the sides of the nose. The effect:

- Up to 30% reduction in nasal airway resistance
- Sustained nasal patency during peak exertion
- No drugs, no rebound congestion, no side effects

The pointed wing tips grip the flared nostril skin instead of pulling at the delicate bridge, giving a more secure hold for runners, cyclists, and combat athletes than a rectangular strip.
